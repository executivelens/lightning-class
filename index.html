Stage 1 · Diagnose

Understand and embrace the issue.

The longest, hardest step — done with radical honesty.

1

Radical objectivity

Audit the feedback. Separate the "tone" from the "text." Even if the delivery is harsh, is there a kernel of truth?

2

The mirror test

Ask: "Am I inadvertently triggering them?" — being too informal, oversharing, or missing small deadlines.

3

Learn from others

Observe peers who earned your boss's trust — and exactly how they communicate.

4

The "user manual" talk

A direct, non-emotional ask: "What is the one thing I could change to make your job easier?"

Stage 2 · Rebuild trust

Neutralize the distrust.

Priority #1: make my boss stop distrusting me.

What doesn't work
  • ✗Venting to coworkers — it gets back to the boss.
  • ✗Making it personal — an emotional battle is already lost.
  • ✗Trying to change their personality instead of your reaction.
What worked
  • ✓Learn the boss's "currency" — data, speed, or loyalty (all three, for Mengxi). Deliver what makes them look good.
  • ✓Over-communicate — proactive updates before they're asked for.
  • ✓Keep a "Win Log" — document contributions and positive feedback.
Stage 2 · The result
We started speaking the same language — data & speed.

She proved herself reliable through proactive communication, and became far more open to my feedback. — Manager's POV

Stage 3 · Build the partnership

Foundation.

The mindset shift
  • →Stopped trying to be liked; focused on being resourceful.
  • →Goal shifted from "proving myself" to making my boss's job easier and more predictable.
  • →Earned trust through my team and partner teams — not just self-promotion.
The steps
  • ✓Navigate up: "When you were in my position, what strategies did you use?" — then let them talk.
  • ✓Sponsorship: which of your boss's peers do you work with — and what's your reputation there?
  • ✓Be the anchor: how would your team describe you to your boss?
  • ✓Be smart about how you share successes.
Stage 3 · Scale the partnership

Scaling.

The mindset shift
  • →Started thinking one step ahead of my boss.
  • →Flagged risks early, brought options, protected their constraints.
  • →Shifted from executor to thought partner.
The steps
  • ✓Internal marketing: build relationships with peers — your work represents your boss.
  • ✓Be your boss's "ambassador": name the common goals, the friction, the "political weather."
  • ✓Anticipate downstream effects: "Since we moved the deadline, Finance might flag Q3 spend — concerned?"
  • ✓Know your boss's constraints — and protect them.
The payoff

From executor to thought partner.

Manager's POV
  • ✓A direct-report spot opened — and I moved her back to being my direct report.
  • ✓I heard about issues through her first, usually with options already on the table.
  • ✓She could foresee problems — whether from people or systems.
  • ✓Leverage her strengths for bigger impact → bigger scope and promotion, done consistently.
